Roman Soldier Bucellatum

Description

Bucellatum was a Roman hardtack biscuit eaten by Roman soliders. It is created by mixing flour, salt, and water. Hardtack, as the name suggests, is very hard, but was a sustainable and portable source of nutrition for roman soldiers.

Ingredients:

  • 2 3/4 cups wholewheat flour
  • 1/3 cup water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tsp butter

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Mix flour, salt and butter. Then add water, whisking in the process to create a whipped dough (the biscuit must be very dry finally)
  2. Roll out the dough and squeeze round (sources say Roman rusks were round).
  3. Make holes in the dough allowing air to come in and out of moisture.
  4. Place the cakes on a tray and place in the oven preheated to 250℃.
  5. Bake rusks for 2.5 hours, shifting them.
  6. Leave the cakes in the oven for a few hours to cool. If the rusks are not completely dry, bake until the expected result.
